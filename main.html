The second largest emirate in the UAE (after Abu Dhabi) is Dubai, founded as a small settlement on the shores of the Persian Gulf in 830 AD. The name of the city comes from the Arabic name for one of the types of locusts common in this region - dibba, namely its diminutive name Dubai. There is an opinion that this choice of name for the city is associated with the numerous populations of locusts that inhabited the territory of the city center at that time.

"Burj Khalifa" (Arabic: برج خليفة‎ - "Khalifa Tower", until 2010 it was called "Burj Dubai" - "Dubai Tower") - a super-tall skyscraper with a height of 828 meters [2] in Dubai (UAE), the highest and the most multi-story building in the world, as well as the tallest structure. The stepped shape of the building resembles a stalagmite.



Burj Al Arab (Arabic: برج العرب‎, literally “Tower of the Arabs”) is a hotel in Dubai, the largest city in the United Arab Emirates[2]. The building stands in the sea at a distance of 280 meters from the shore on an artificial island connected to the land by a bridge. With a height of 321 m, it was the tallest hotel in the world until the construction of the Rose Tower Hotel, 333 m high, which opened in April 2008. The Burj Al Arab is currently the 5th tallest hotel in the world, although 39% of its total height is unoccupied space[3][4][5]. As of 2015, the Burj Al Arab is the 56th tallest building in Asia and the 70th tallest in the world.



The Palm Jumeirah is an archipelago of artificial islands on the Persian Gulf in Jumeirah, Dubai, United Arab Emirates. It is part of a larger series of developments called the Palm Islands, including Palm Jebel Ali and Palm Deira, which, if completed, will together increase Dubai's shoreline by a total of 520 kilometers (320 mi).[1] It has an estimated population of at least 10,000 as of 2017.[2][needs update]
